## Task 4: The Bonsall Lecture — Pitch to Alex

Draft a pitch memo to Alex (Department Head) proposing the creation of a named lecture series: **The Bonsall Lecture**.

**Concept:**
- A named annual lecture honoring Bonsall S. Wilton '72 and his investment in undergraduate education
- The Department Head selects a speaker each year
- The speaker delivers a 1-hour presentation to the incoming freshman biology class
- The talk should blend science and personal journey — "science and their life" — to inspire new students and build scientific identity early
- This is a prestige appointment: being asked to give The Bonsall Lecture is an honor

**Why this matters:**
- It gives freshmen a powerful early experience connecting them to the department and to science as a vocation
- It creates a recurring, visible way to honor Wilton's legacy
- It gives the DH a meaningful lever for recognizing faculty excellence
- It becomes a department tradition and recruiting tool
- It connects to the CUREs mission: if students hear an inspiring talk their first week, they're more likely to engage deeply in CURE research

**Format:** Short, persuasive memo. 1 page max. Conversational but professional — this is an internal pitch to a colleague, not a formal proposal.
